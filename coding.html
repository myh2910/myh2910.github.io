IMO 2020/3

Solution to the problem 3 of IMO 2020
There are \(4n\) pebbles of weights \(1,2,3,\dots,4n\). Each pebble is coloured in one of \(n\) colours and there are four pebbles of each colour. Show that we can arrange the pebbles into two piles so that the following two conditions are both satisfied:
  • The total weights of both piles are the same.
  • Each pile contains two pebbles of each colour.
IMO 2020/3 (Proposed by Milan Haiman, Hungary and Carl Schildkraut, USA)
in spanish. (Source code)

IMO 2018/1

Visual solution to the problem 1 of IMO 2018
Let \(\Gamma\) be the circumcircle of acute-angled triangle \(ABC\). Points \(D\) and \(E\) lie on segments \(AB\) and \(AC\), respectively, such that \(AD=AE\). The perpendicular bisectors of \(BD\) and \(CE\) intersect the minor arcs \(AB\) and \(AC\) of \(\Gamma\) at points \(F\) and \(G\), respectively. Prove that the lines \(DE\) and \(FG\) are parallel (or are the same line).

IMO 2018/1 (Proposed by Silouanos Brazitikos, Vangelis Psyxas and Michael Sarantis, Greece)
in spanish. (Source code)

IMO 2020/1

Visual solution to the problem 1 of IMO 2020
Consider the convex quadrilateral \(ABCD\). The point \(P\) is in the interior of \(ABCD\). The following ratio equalities hold: \[\angle PAD:\angle PBA:\angle DPA=1:2:3=\angle CBP:\angle BAP:\angle BPC.\] Prove that the following three lines meet in a point: the internal bisectors of angles \(\angle ADP\) and \(\angle PCB\) and the perpendicular bisector of segment \(AB\).

IMO 2020/1 (Proposed by Dominik Burek, Poland)
in spanish. (Source code)

IMO 2015/4

Visual solution to the problem 4 of IMO 2015
Triangle \(ABC\) has circumcircle \(\Omega\) and circumcentre \(O\). A circle \(\Gamma\) with centre \(A\) intersects the segment \(BC\) at points \(D\) and \(E\), such that \(B\), \(D\), \(E\), and \(C\) are all different and lie on line \(BC\) in this order. Let \(F\) and \(G\) be the points of intersection of \(\Gamma\) and \(\Omega\), such that \(A\), \(F\), \(B\), \(C\), and \(G\) lie on \(\Omega\) in this order. Let \(K\) be the second point of intersection of the circumcircle of triangle \(BDF\) and the segment \(AB\). Let \(L\) be the second point of intersection of the circumcircle of triangle \(CGE\) and the segment \(CA\).

Suppose that the lines \(FK\) and \(GL\) are different and intersect at the point \(X\). Prove that \(X\) lies on the line \(AO\).

IMO 2015/4 (Proposed by Silouanos Brazitikos, Greece)
in spanish. (Source code)
