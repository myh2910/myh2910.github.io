Math Olympiad Theorems

This is a list of random theorems.

Mean Value Theorem

Let \(f:[a,b]\to\RR\) be a continuous function on the closed interval \([a,b]\), and differentiable on the open interval \((a,b)\), where \(a<b\). Then there exists some \(c\) in \((a,b)\) such that \[f'(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}.\]

Erd≈ës–Ginzburg–Ziv Theorem

Any multiset of \(2n-1\) integers has a subset of size \(n\) the sum of whose elements is a multiple of \(n\), but that the same is not true of multisets of size \(2n-2\).

Chevalley–Warning Theorem

Let \(p\) be a prime and let \(k\) and \(n\) be positive integers. Let \(f_1,\dots,f_k\) be polynomials with integer coefficients in the variables \(X_1,\dots,X_n\), such that \[n>\sum_{i=1}^k\deg{f_i}.\] Then the number of \(n-\)tuples \((x_1,\dots,x_n)\in\{0,1,\dots,p-1\}^n\) such that \[f_1(x_1,\dots,x_n)\equiv f_2(x_1,\dots,x_n)\equiv\cdots\equiv f_k(x_1,\dots,x_n)\equiv 0\pmod{p}\] is a multiple of \(p\).
